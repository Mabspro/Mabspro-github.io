Mining Sector in Zambia

Overview

Zambia is Africa's second-largest copper producer and has significant deposits of other minerals including cobalt, gold, and emeralds. The mining sector is a crucial component of Zambia's economy, contributing significantly to export earnings and employment.

Key Minerals

  • Copper
  • Cobalt
  • Gold
  • Emeralds
  • Manganese

Investment Opportunities

  • Exploration and extraction of minerals
  • Value-added processing and manufacturing
  • Mining equipment and services
  • Green mining technologies

Mining Cadastre Portal

The Ministry of Mines and Minerals Development has launched the Mining Cadastre Portal, allowing for efficient management of mineral rights and applications. Use the portal to:

  • Submit reports and make payments online
  • Apply for new licenses or renewals
  • Track your license portfolio with an interactive map

Visit the portal at https://portal.miningcadastre.gov.zm.

Mining Policies and Regulations

Zambia has implemented several policies to attract investment in the mining sector, including:

  • Competitive tax regime
  • Streamlined licensing procedures
  • Protection of investor rights
  • Environmental regulations promoting sustainable mining practices

Recent Developments

  • Increased focus on diversification beyond copper
  • Growing interest in rare earth elements
  • Emphasis on local content and skills development
  • Adoption of new technologies for improved efficiency and sustainability
