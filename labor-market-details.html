Labor Market Details

Overview

Zambia's labor market is characterized by a young and growing workforce, with a mix of formal and informal employment. The country faces challenges in job creation and skills development but also presents opportunities for businesses seeking a capable workforce.

Key Statistics

  • Total population: Approximately 18.9 million (2021 estimate)
  • Working-age population (15-64 years): About 52% of the total population
  • Labor force participation rate: 69.7%
  • Unemployment rate: 12.5% (2021 estimate)
  • Youth unemployment rate: 22.6% (15-24 years)

Employment by Sector

  • Agriculture: 48.9%
  • Services: 43.5%
  • Industry: 7.6%

Education and Skills

  • Literacy rate: 86.7% (2018 estimate)
  • Primary school enrollment: 98%
  • Secondary school enrollment: 45%
  • Tertiary education enrollment: 4%
  • Technical and Vocational Education and Training (TVET) institutions: Over 300

Labor Laws and Regulations

  • Minimum wage: Varies by sector, ranging from ZMW 1,000 to ZMW 2,400 per month
  • Standard work week: 48 hours
  • Paid annual leave: 24 working days per year
  • Social security contributions: Employer 5%, Employee 5%
  • Key legislation: Employment Code Act of 2019

Skills in Demand

  • Information and Communication Technology (ICT)
  • Engineering and technical skills
  • Agriculture and agribusiness
  • Financial services and accounting
  • Healthcare professionals
  • Mining and geological expertise

Challenges

  • Skills mismatch between education system output and industry needs
  • High youth unemployment
  • Large informal sector
  • Limited job creation in formal sectors
  • Brain drain of skilled professionals

Government Initiatives

  • National Youth Policy and Action Plan for Youth Empowerment and Employment
  • Skills Development Fund
  • Technical Education, Vocational and Entrepreneurship Training (TEVET) Policy
  • Zambia Education Enhancement Project
  • Graduate internship programs

Opportunities for Employers

  • Access to a young, trainable workforce
  • Partnerships with educational institutions for tailored training programs
  • Government incentives for job creation and skills development
  • Potential for productivity gains through workforce development

Future Outlook

Zambia's labor market presents both challenges and opportunities. The country's young population and increasing focus on education and skills development offer potential for a dynamic workforce. Employers who invest in training and development can benefit from a growing pool of talent, while contributing to the country's economic growth and social development.
