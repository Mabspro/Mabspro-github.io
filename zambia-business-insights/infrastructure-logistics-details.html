Infrastructure and Logistics Details

Overview

Zambia's infrastructure and logistics sector is crucial for the country's economic development and regional integration. The government has prioritized infrastructure development to enhance connectivity, reduce transportation costs, and improve the overall business environment.

Road Network

  • Total road network: approximately 67,671 km
  • Paved roads: about 14,888 km
  • Major highways: Great North Road, Great East Road, Lusaka-Mongu Road
  • Ongoing projects: Link Zambia 8000, Pave Zambia 2000

Railway System

  • Two main railway systems: Zambia Railways Limited and TAZARA
  • Total rail network: approximately 2,922 km
  • Key routes: Copperbelt to Livingstone, Kapiri Mposhi to Dar es Salaam (Tanzania)

Airports

  • Major international airports: Kenneth Kaunda International Airport (Lusaka), Simon Mwansa Kapwepwe International Airport (Ndola)
  • Domestic airports: Livingstone, Mfuwe, Solwezi, and others
  • Ongoing upgrades to improve capacity and facilities

Ports and Inland Waterways

  • Major inland port: Mpulungu Port on Lake Tanganyika
  • Access to sea ports through neighboring countries: Dar es Salaam (Tanzania), Beira and Nacala (Mozambique), Walvis Bay (Namibia)
  • Waterways on lakes and rivers for local transportation

Telecommunications

  • Growing mobile phone penetration: over 19 million subscribers
  • Increasing internet usage: approximately 10 million users
  • Major telecom operators: MTN Zambia, Airtel Zambia, Zamtel
  • Ongoing fiber optic network expansion

Energy Infrastructure

  • Major power plants: Kariba North Bank, Kafue Gorge, Victoria Falls
  • Transmission network: over 11,000 km of high voltage lines
  • Ongoing projects to increase generation capacity and improve distribution

Investment Opportunities

  • Public-Private Partnerships (PPPs) in infrastructure development
  • Road construction and maintenance
  • Railway rehabilitation and expansion
  • Airport modernization and management
  • Telecommunications infrastructure and services
  • Logistics and supply chain solutions

Challenges and Future Outlook

While Zambia has made significant progress in infrastructure development, challenges remain, including funding constraints and maintenance of existing infrastructure. The government's continued focus on infrastructure improvement, coupled with increasing regional integration and private sector participation, presents numerous opportunities for growth and investment in this sector.
