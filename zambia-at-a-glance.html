Zambia at a Glance

Country Overview

Zambia is a landlocked country in Southern Africa, known for its diverse wildlife, vast copper reserves, and vibrant culture. With a stable political environment and pro-business policies, Zambia offers numerous opportunities for investors across various sectors.

Key Facts

Capital

Lusaka

Population

Approximately 18 million (2021 estimate)

Official Language

English

Currency

Zambian Kwacha (ZMW)

Government

Presidential Republic

Major Industries

Mining, Agriculture, Tourism, Manufacturing

Economic Highlights

  • Africa's second-largest copper producer
  • Rich in natural resources including minerals, forests, and wildlife
  • Growing agricultural sector with potential for expansion
  • Emerging renewable energy market
  • Strategic location with access to eight neighboring countries

Business Environment

  • Pro-investment government policies
  • Competitive tax regime
  • Access to regional markets through trade agreements
  • Young and growing workforce
  • Improving infrastructure and connectivity

Investment Opportunities

Zambia offers diverse investment opportunities across various sectors, including:

  • Mining and mineral processing
  • Commercial agriculture and agro-processing
  • Renewable energy projects
  • Tourism and hospitality
  • Manufacturing and value addition
  • Information and Communication Technology (ICT)
